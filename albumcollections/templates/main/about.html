Digital Record Shelves for your Spotify Albums

A metaphor for the purpose of this app

Imagine Spotify's role in your music listening experience in classic physical terms.

It is the record store (a very extensive and inexpensive one), the record player (with great features like turning the record over for you), and your friend telling you what new stuff to check out (who knows you weirdly well).

Spotify is also your strangely constructed record cabinet, providing you with infinitely customizable shelves to store your Singles but only lending a single shelf with the appropriate dimensions to store your LPs.

Album Collections is a project that seeks to fill this organizational gap. It represents additional customizable shelves -- collections -- in your digital record cabinet. Whenever you're browsing the record store, you can take an album home with you to add to one of your collections. Then you can look through the albums on your shelves, rearrange them, shuffle them up and take them to the record player

The Mixtape has had a fantastic modern representation for a long time. This app introduces the concept of playlists for your albums, bringing album collection into the music subscription age.

What is a collection?

Any playlist in your Spotify account can be represented in this app as a collection. Here's an example of one. Within a collection, you can view the contents and link to the corresponding Spotify playlist and contained Spotify albums. If you are logged in and own the playlist (free or premium account), then you can reorder (as long the full track list is present) and remove albums from the playlist.

Guide

Each section goes into detail on specific capabilities of the app. They are labeled with icons based on which application -- Album Collections and/or Spotify -- will be involved in the task. Note that as a prerequisite you will need to have some playlists in your Spotify account with music (either full albums or individual tracks) that you want to add as collections. You could also create some new empty playlists to start, and then add music after you've added them as collections.

Install

Album Collections is a progressive web app. Install it on your device/s via a browser for the smoothest experience. Offline capabilities are not currently supported but are a possibility in the future.

Login

Press the Login button on the navbar or main page to authenticate with your Spotify account. This will allow you to view your Spotify playlists as collections and also enable collection playing and modifying.

Add a Collection

Any playlists that you have added in your spotify account can be added as a collection via the form on the main page (once logged in). The end result of the addition process is that another playlist of yours will be accessible as a collection by clicking on it's "shelf" on the main page. There are a couple options when adding a collection:

  1. Create a copy of the source playlist to add as a collection. This could be helpful if the source playlist is owned by someone else, because the copy will be part of your account and thus enable reorder and remove actions from the collection. It also could be helpul in conjunction with the below option of filling in missing album tracks, so that the original playlist is not modified.
  2. Fill in missing album tracks. Any tracks missing from their respective albums will be inserted into the playlist. An album in the playlist is only movable within the collection when all tracks are present, in the correct order and not separated by tracks from other albums. This option ensures that this is the case for all albums by modifying the playlist.

Remove Collections

The collections that you have added via the above step can be removed as well, also from the main page (when logged in). This will only remove the collection from view in Albums Collections, it won't have any affect on the backing Spotify playlists.

Collections can also be removed from view by deleting the underlying playlist in Spotify.

Add album/s to a Collection

To add albums that aren't in any of your collections already to a collection, use Spotify. Navigate to the album you'd like to add to the collection. In the options for the album, select Add to playlist, and choose the playlist corresponding to the collection you would like it to be a part of. Alternatively in the desktop Spotify App, you can utilize the drag n' drop functionality to drag the album/s into the desired playlist.

You can add or complete albums already present in one of your collections from this app. From your collection, click on the album you want to add and then press the button and choose a destination collection. This is a good way to move albums between collections, or fill in missing tracks in the album in the collection playlist to 'complete' it. This will not add a duplicate if an album already exists in an album. If that is desired, please use the Spotify app to do so.

View your Collections

The main page on Album Collections displays the collections that you have added. Click on a collection to open it. The collection page contains interactable cover art for the contained albums, and has a collection control bar at the bottom that shows the name of collection, along with several buttons to shufle, reorder, or access it in Spotify.

You can also view any playlist in Spotify as a collection by manually inputting it's playlist id in the address bar.

Reorder a Collection

The order of the albums in the collection can be changed via drag n' drop. This functionality can be toggled using the button. The order of the collection on the collection page reflects that order of the containing playlist.

Shuffle a Collection

Press on the button to randomly change the order of the albums in the collection. Under the hood this recreates the playlist using the album tracks already in the collection. Note that because of this shuffling strategy, items that aren't music tracks (ex: podcast episodes) will be removed from the playlist. If you'd like to retain an original source playlist containing podcast episodes then please create a copy of the playlist when adding the collection.

Remove an Album from a Collection

Click on the album you would like to remove from the collection. Then press the button, and respond to the 'are you sure' prompt. This will remove all songs from this album from the containing playlist, and remove the album art image from the screen.
