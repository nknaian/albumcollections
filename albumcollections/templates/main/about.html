Digital record shelves

A metaphor for how to pair this app with your Spotify account

Imagine a digital ecosystem of vinyl records. Spotify is both the record store (a very extensive and inexpensive one) and the record player (with great features like turning the record over for you).

Spotify provides some organization options for your records, but they're very limited; you only have one shelf to store all your records, and only a select few ways to order them.

Album Collections is a project that seeks to fill this organizational gap. It represents additional customizable shelves -- otherwise known as "collections" -- in your record cabinet. Whenever you're browsing the record store (Spotify), you can take an album home with you to add to one of your collections (by adding to a Spotify playlist). Then you can look through the albums on your shelves, rearrange them, pick out one to play, or even stack them on your record player to spin one after the other.

This sort of functionality has always existed in Spotify for individual tracks -- the Mixtape has had a fantastic digital representation for a long time. This app introduces the concept of playlists for your albums, bringing album collection into the music subscription age.
